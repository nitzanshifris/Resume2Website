# User Intent Specification

*Defining what users want to achieve and why they choose CV2WEB*

## Primary User Intents

### UI01: Escape the "No" Pile
**User Need**: "I want my application to get noticed and not immediately discarded"

**Context**: Traditional CVs often get 6-10 seconds of attention before being filtered out. Users need a format that captures attention immediately and tells their story compellingly.

**Success Criteria**: 
- Portfolio formats highlight key achievements within first 3 seconds of viewing
- Visual hierarchy guides recruiter attention to strongest qualifications
- Mobile-optimized presentation for recruiters reviewing on phones

### UI02: Tell Complete Career Story
**User Need**: "I want to showcase all my relevant experience, not just what fits on one page"

**Context**: Traditional CV formats force artificial constraints. Users have projects, achievements, and context that deserve proper presentation.

**Success Criteria**:
- All CV information preserved and presented logically
- Related experiences grouped meaningfully  
- Achievements contextualized with metrics and impact
- No information lost due to format constraints

### UI03: Professional Credibility
**User Need**: "I want to look professional and competent to hiring managers"

**Context**: Users lack design skills but need professional presentation. They want to be judged on qualifications, not formatting ability.

**Success Criteria**:
- Generated portfolios meet corporate presentation standards
- Consistent, polished appearance across all sections
- Typography and layout comparable to professionally designed materials
- Error-free presentation of all content

### UI04: Authentic Self-Representation  
**User Need**: "I want my personality and voice to come through, not generic templating"

**Context**: Users want to stand out while remaining professional. Their unique language and perspective are differentiators.

**Success Criteria**:
- Original language preserved exactly as written
- Personal achievements and projects highlighted appropriately
- Industry-specific terminology maintained
- Cultural and linguistic authenticity respected

## User Journey Intents

### UI05: Immediate Value
**User Need**: "I want to see results quickly without complex setup"

**Context**: Job searching is urgent. Users need immediate value to justify time investment.

**Success Criteria**:
- Completed portfolio viewable within 5 minutes
- No account creation required for initial use
- Clear progress indication throughout process
- Immediate preview of transformed content

### UI06: Control and Iteration
**User Need**: "I want to refine and improve my portfolio over time"

**Context**: Users discover new ways to present themselves as they receive feedback and apply for different roles.

**Success Criteria**:
- Easy editing of extracted information
- Multiple template options for different industries
- Version control for different applications
- Quick regeneration with changes

### UI07: Confidence Building
**User Need**: "I want to feel confident about my applications"

**Context**: Job searching is inherently anxiety-inducing. Users need reassurance that their presentation is competitive.

**Success Criteria**:
- Visual transformation builds user confidence
- Clear articulation of their value proposition  
- Professional appearance reduces anxiety about presentation
- Comparison features show improvement over original CV

## Specialized User Intents

### UI08: Career Transition Support
**User Need**: "I want to present transferable skills when changing careers"

**Context**: Career changers struggle to connect past experience to new roles. Traditional CVs don't tell transition stories well.

**Success Criteria**:
- Skills section emphasizes transferable capabilities
- Project work highlighted equally with employment
- Education and certifications given appropriate weight
- Story flows logically despite industry changes

### UI09: International Accessibility  
**User Need**: "I want my portfolio to work regardless of my CV format or language level"

**Context**: International users have varying CV conventions and may be non-native English speakers.

**Success Criteria**:
- Support for international CV formats and conventions
- Preservation of non-English names and terminology
- Accommodation of different date formats and address styles
- No bias toward specific cultural presentation norms

### UI10: Privacy and Control
**User Need**: "I want control over my information and how it's used"

**Context**: CV data is highly personal and sensitive. Users need trust and transparency.

**Success Criteria**:
- Clear understanding of data usage and retention
- Ability to delete information permanently
- No surprise sharing or secondary use of data
- Transparent processing with explainable outcomes

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### UI11: False Enhancement
**Never**: Modify, improve, or embellish user's original content
**Why**: Users' authentic voice is their competitive advantage

### UI12: Generic Templating
**Never**: Apply cookie-cutter formatting that makes all portfolios look identical
**Why**: Users need to differentiate themselves in competitive markets

### UI13: Information Loss
**Never**: Omit information due to template limitations or aesthetic preferences
**Why**: Every detail might be relevant to the right opportunity

### UI14: Complexity Creep
**Never**: Require extensive user input or configuration to achieve basic functionality
**Why**: Users need immediate value with minimal effort

---

*These user intents drive all product decisions. When evaluating features or changes, always ask: "Does this serve these core user needs?"*
